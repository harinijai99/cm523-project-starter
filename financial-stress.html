Financial Stress

Money is a common source of stress for the average college student. whether it's worrying about paying rent or feeling bogged down by education debt, tuition expenses or the lack of financial aid. Financial stress in students is linked to several underlying mental health issues including anxiety, depression, behavioural changes and social withdrawal. Given here are some unique tactics to cope with the financial stress affecting you.

  • Budgeting: In simple words refers to planning your incomes and expenses inorder to placate any financial strain affecting your mental health. Given below are resources and tips which will help you budget better.
    • Step1: Identify your sources of income
    • Step 2: Estimate your expenses
    • Step 3: Adjust your income and expenses, inorder to not have a negative balance. An effective way to do this is follow the 50/30/20 rule, which calls for 50% of your income be directed towards your needs, 30% towards your wants and 20% towards savings.
    • Some helpful tools to budget are: Google Sheets, Apps such as Mint, Every Dollor, You need a budget,
  • Scholarships & Grants: One of the most useful resource to reduce your financial burden is to make use of scholarships and grants. Scholarships are frequently given to students on the basis of merit and are given to those who have proved academic proficiency or other exceptional accomplishments, such as athletic or creative achievements. Grants, on the other hand, are frequently given to students who can prove they have a financial need. given below are links to official government resources which show the possible options available. You can also contact your university's 'Financial Aid Officer' for oppurtunities within your school.
