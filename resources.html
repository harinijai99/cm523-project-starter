Resources

We have some additional resources which will guide you to the next step of your mental health journey!

  • Disaster Distress Helpline - Call or text 1-800-985-5990: This helpline provides immediate crisis counseling for people who are experiencing emotional distress or troubling thoughts due to a natural or human made disasters. This helpline is confidential, multilingual, and available 24/7 for those in need.
  • MoodGym : This is a 24 hour toll-free crisis hotline that can help those at risk, by putting them into contact with a local crisis center that helps people find immediate support and prevention tactics nearby.
  • Recovery International :This self help mental health organization organizes weekly peer-led support groups and meetings for multiple communities. They also have phone and internet based meetings to support various disorders.
  • NeedyMeds :This is a national non-profit organization that helps those who suffer from mental health conditions to obtain affordable care. Needymeds people secure the required treatment and medication through a database of assistance programs, free clinics and coupons.
  • American Foundation for Suicide Preventionl : The AFSP was built to prevent suicide, as well as to provide support to those who have or have almost lost someone they love to suicide. With a presence in all 50 states, the AFSP advocates for life.
  • Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance: : The DBSA was created for those who suffer from mood disorders and aims provide wellness support for those living with depression and bipolar disorder, through the provision of wellness counselling and support groups.
  • The Trevor Project : The Trevor project provides support to those in the LGBTQIA+ community youth, who are struggling with various mental health disorders. The project has a 24/7 hotline, through call and text and a variety of support groups and resources to those in the community.
  • Anxiety and Depression Association of America : The ADA provides a comprehensive database for support groups surrounding any and all mental health disorders. The database also helps users find support groups near them by entering their area code and state.
  • Crisis Text Line :Text 741-741 : The Crisis text line offourse free text support for anyone in the U.S.A. Counsellors are available on the helplines 24/7 to help those suffering from any mental health condition. These counsellors are those who have received extensive training and support. various disorders.
  • International OCD foundation : The international OCD foundation aims to end the stigma surrounding obsessive compulsive disorder. It provides resources and treatment facilities for those who are in need, through online and phone support groups.
  • Support Groups Central : Support group central rings together people from over 120 countries worldwide, who are struggling with various mental health conditions. This online platform hasgroup meetings, one-on-one sessions and virtual support groups that help people get started on their mental health journey. The very nature of this online support group also helps people maintain their anonymity.
  • 7 cups : This is considered to be the worldâ€™s largest emotional support system. 7 cups is a smartphone app that offers emotional support by connecting people with listeners and therapists. The app allows those in distress to chat 1-1 with with a listener or a therapist, and even provides a private chat room, as well as community support groups accessible online.
  • SMART recovery : An online support group that helps people combat addictions. SMART recovery helps those struggling with substance abuse and OCD combat their addictions through a focused support group.
  • Mental Health America : This is a non-profit organization that offers interventions, preventative treatments and other mental health support services. Mental Health America has an online support group hosted by Inspire, that helps people with anxiety and depression, and other mental health conditions.
