Academic Issues: Financial Stress: Budgeting

Here's a tactic to help you reduce your financial stress!

Money is a common source of stress for the average college student. whether it's worrying about paying rent or feeling bogged down by education debt, tuition expenses or the lack of financial aid. Financial stress in students is linked to several underlying mental health issues including anxiety, depression, behavioural changes and social withdrawal. The tactic suggested here for you to help you tackle this problem is 'Budgeting'
Budgeting: In simple words refers to planning your incomes and expenses inorder to placate any financial strain affecting your mental health. Given below are resources and tips which will help you budget better.
  • Step1: Identify your sources of income
  • Step 2: Estimate your expenses
  • Step 3: Adjust your income and expenses, inorder to not have a negative balance. An effective way to do this is follow the 50/30/20 rule, which calls for 50% of your income be directed towards your needs, 30% towards your wants and 20% towards savings.
