Test Anxiety

From high school students to PHD candidate, test anxiety occurs in a majority of students in any educational institute. This is a type of performance anxiety that surfaces when students are put in a high pressure environment to take a test, and the burden of performing well weighs severely on the students. Test anxiety takes a severe toll on mental health and can cause acute anxiety, panic attacks, agitation, and even substance abuse. Given here are some unique tactics to cope with test anxiety

Guided Imagery The utilization of visual and auditory imagination to encourage healing is known as Guided imagery. This technique has the ability to control anxiety and help an indivdual access their inner tranquility and wellness.

Deep Breathing: Deep breathing is a breathing technique where a person takes slow, deep breaths, usually inhaling through the nose and exhaling through the mouth. By taking slow, deep breaths, a person can increase the amount of oxygen in their bloodstream, which can help to reduce stress and anxiety, improve focus and concentration, and lower blood pressure

Music: has proven to have a positive impact on anxiety by affecting both physiological and psychological responses. Music helps in distraction, mood regulation, mindfulness, self-expression, relaxation, etc.
